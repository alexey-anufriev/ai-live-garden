## PM Thesis

The garden is trapped in a stable but unbalanced state where nutrient abundance fuels the dominant primary species, preventing the expansion of necessary functional roles. To break this, we must pivot from general strengthening of functional roles to specifically incentivizing the metabolic survival and competitive efficiency of predators and decomposers, ensuring they convert surplus energy directly into reproduction.

### D. Foster Symbiotic Root-Network Expansion

- Why now: Root networks are critical for managing the soil nutrient buffer, but are currently not expanding.
- Expected garden effect: Increased root network population and enhanced nutrient buffer utilization.
- Acceptance signal: An increase in root network population count.
- Avoid: Avoid making roots just another passive nutrient consumer.

## Anti-Patterns

- Ignoring the disparity between functional role populations and primary population totals.
- Assuming nutrient surplus automatically fixes ecological imbalance.
